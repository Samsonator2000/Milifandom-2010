Constitution of the Republic

ARTICLE 1:~Institutions of Government

The Republic is led by The President of the Republic.
The Republic has 2 legislative bodies, the House of Commons, and the Senate of The Republic.
Elections for President of the Republic must be held at least once per month, at the same time as elections to the Senate. All members of the Republic may vote in elections for President.
All citizens of the Republic are members of the House of Commons, unless they are the President, or a member of the Senate.
The Senate of the Republic has 3 members. Elections to the Senate must be held at least once per month, at the same time as elections for President. All senators are elected at the same vote. All members of the Republic may vote in elections to the Senate.
The President may not be a member of the House of Commons or the Senate of the Republic.

ARTICLE 2:~Powers of the House of Commons

Any member of the House of Commons may propose a new bill to vote on. Any other member may propose changes to the bill. If the bill is supported by a majority in the House of Commons, it must be voted on by the Senate.

ARTICLE 3:~Powers of the Senate

The Senate must review, and then vote on any bills that are supported by a majority in the House of Commons.
If the Senate amends the bill, it must be voted on by the House of Commons again.
If the bill is supported by a majority of senators, it must be given assent by the President.


The Senate must elect a new President of the Senate immediately after a Senate election. In the event of a tie, the President of the Republic chooses the President of the Senate. The President of the Senate is also the Vice President of the Republic. The President of the Senate must be a member of the Senate.

The President of the Senate must immediately appoint one other Senator as Chief Justice, and the other as Secretary of State.

The Chief Justice must decide the guilt of any citizen accused of breaking the law. They may delegate this power to a jury.

If the citizen is found guilty, the Chief Justice must give the the stated sentence for the law or laws broken.

The Secretary of State must represent the Republic in any interactions with other nations.

The Secretary of State is responsible for negotiating and amending international legislation on behalf of the Republic.

The Secretary of State must deliver the final decision of the Republic on international legislation.

ARTICLE 4:~Powers of the President

The President has the power to give presidential assent to any bill which is supported by a majority in both the House of Commons and the Senate. A bill which has received presidential assent becomes law, and must be enforced by the President.

The President may appoint secretaries. These secretaries must be members of the House of Commons. They may be given executive power over:
- Policing
- Public Services
- State Property
- Trade
- Currency
- State Events
- Official State Symbols
All executive powers not delegated to secretaries are held by the President.

If the President is deemed unable to carry out his duties by a unanimous vote of the Senate, the Vice President may carry out the powers of his role until the next presidential election.

ARTICLE 5:~Rights of Citizens

No citizen of the Republic may be prevented from voting in elections.

No citizen of the Republic who is not a member of the Senate, or the President, may be prevented from sitting in the House of Commons.

All citizens have the right to farm the common land.

All citizens have the right to carry a weapon crafted from wood, to defend their person and property.
