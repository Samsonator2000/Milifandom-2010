Spiritual Commune

Constitution of the Spiritual Commune

ARTICLE 1:~Clergy

citizens of the Commune are either monks or priests.

The Commune is led by 3 priests, the longest serving of which is the High Priest.

The High Priest is in charge of maintaining peace on the Holy Mountain.

The High Priest must appoint one priest to be the Grand Missionary, and represent the Spiritual Commune in foreign relations, and deliver the decision of the priests on international legislation.

The High Priest must appoint the other priest as Grand Defender, to lead the guard, and protect the Holy Mountain from foreign threats.

ARTICLE 2:~Sin

The three cardinal sins are as follows:
- Thou shalt not kill a living being upon the Holy Mountain, unless the blade is of gold.
- Thou shalt not kill a monk or priest who is in prayer.
- Thou shalt not burn the trees on the Holy Mountain.


If a monk commits a sin, they must repent by throwing themselves from the mountain, and drowning themselves in the river.

If a priest commits a sin, they must forfeit their priesthood, and the High Priest will select a new priest.

New sins may be established, or disestablished, by a unanimous vote of the three priests. The three cardinal sins may not be altered
