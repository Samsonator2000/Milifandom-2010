The League of Nations

Constitution of the League of Nations

ARTICLE 1~Legislation

Each state has one vote in the League of Nations. They are represented in the League of Nations as their individual constitutions outline.

All legislation passed by the League of Nations is enforceable by server admin powers and requires a unanimous vote by the League of Nations to create.

League of Nations international legislation can never contradict a nation's constitution, or this constitution.

International legislation may be repealed by a unanimous vote of the League of Nations.

ARTICLE 2~Territory

Borders are inviolable. The territory of each nation can be expanded by a unanimous vote from the League of Nations. All parties must see the new border, drawn on a level 2 map, and agree on the new border as displayed in the image.

Theft, murder and damage to property against the citizens of a nation within their own borders by a member of a different nation is forbidden.

ARTICLE 3~War

All countries have the right to declare war against any other nation. War cannot be fought over borders that have already been established. War occurs outside of the nations' borders.

ARTICLE 4~Presidency

The president decides the dates and times of votes. Any suggestion for a vote must be scheduled for within a week, and be open for at least 24 hours.

The presidency rotates on the first of each month. The Republic holds the presidency first, followed by the Monarchy, the Democratic State and then the Spiritual Commune.
